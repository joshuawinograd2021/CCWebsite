Meet the Team


Evan is an Economics and International Studies student passionate about the connection between entrepreneurship and social change. When not working for Community Currency, he can be found helping out with a microfinance group called LEND or Students Consulting for Nonprofit Organizations (SCNO) on the campus of Northwestern University. To relax, Evan loves playing sports, watching sports, arguing about sports, or hiking near his hometown of Olympia, Washington.
Jackson is from Long Beach, California and is an undergraduate student at Northwestern University. He engages in company research, helping all of the teams accomplish their goals as well as reach out to corporations for potential partnerships. During his free time, Jackson enjoys watching Netflix and cooking. His favorite thing to do is play sports with his friends.
Since making the trip up to Evanston from his hometown of Raleigh, North Carolina to attend Northwestern University, Jason has played on the club soccer team, produced at the local television station, and worked on the marketing team for the school newspaper. Majoring in Economics and Geography, Jason manages the finances of the company. When he's not crunching numbers, Jason loves to go to the outer banks and play or watch almost any sport.
Justin is from Barrington, Illinois, a suburb of Chicago. Since coming to Northwestern University, Justin has worked for TAMID consulting group, The entrepreneurship team for one of the school’s largest business groups, and is a member of Delta Tau Delta fraternity. As an economics major, Justin is the secretary of the company. When Justin is not busy with school work, he loves playing basketball.
Meghan is a native Texan and Northwestern undergraduate studying Journalism, Legal Studies and Global Health. She admits it’s an odd mix of studies, but she loves exploring and reporting on topics like international women’s health. Outside of academics and her role in Community Currency, Meghan is passionate about comedy and sketch television; she’s always writing screenplays, watching sketches or working on film sets. You can find her working as the 1st Assistant Director of Northwestern Sketch Television or as the Story Director for the Nine Lives Podcast on NU’s campus. If she’s not doing any of the above, she’s probably eating some type of bread or cheese.
Jordan is from Nottingham, England. He was a participant on the renowned Sutton Trust US Programme and is pursuing a double major in Radio/Tv/Film and Computer Science at Northwestern. Outside of Community Currency, Jordan spends his time operating as the Technical Director for Nine Lives Podcast. He also has a weekly radio show on WNUR and is actively involved in Northwestern University’s Dance Marathon. In his spare time, he enjoys watching soccer, drinking tea and Facetiming his younger siblings back home.
Lauren is a Manufacturing and Design Engineering student passionate about both design and humanitarian work. She is Community Currency’s main product designer as well as a member of Engineers Without Borders, Best Buddies, Alpha Phi Sorority, and an aspiring photographer. She has worked with both the Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago and Northwestern University as a designer and consultant on engineering projects since starting at Northwestern in 2015. When all of the above are said and done, she is most likely asleep or talking to her dog.
Zach is a student at Northwestern University interested in economic development and global health. Outside of his role in Community Currency, Zach manages student-run businesses on campus through Northwestern Student Holdings, and also allocates funds to student organizations as a member of the Associated Student Government. In his free time, Zach enjoys playing tennis and eating a nice steak.
Robinson Markus is a Northwestern junior passionate about creating for a more socially just world. Last summer, he & Jackson took a $10,000 grant to become the first nonprofit in Northwestern’s summer startup accelerator. Outside of Community Currency, Robinson works as an Ambassador for Democracy Earth – a blockchain-based nonprofit organization. He loves documentary filmmaking, tennis, meditation, and reading anything you put in his hands.
Julia is third year undergraduate student majoring in Human Development and Psychological Services and is planning to attend medical school after graduating from Northwestern. She is interested in exploring how social and psychological aspects influence health and is passionate about making a change in her community. In addition to being a graphic designer for Community Currency, she is an active member of Alpha Phi Sorority and Phi Delta Epsilon Fraternity, as well as the Director of Marketing for Relay For Life. On campus, she also works as a research assistant in Northwestern’s Lifespan Development Lab and the Aphasia and Neurolinguistics Research Lab. When she's not busy with school, Julia likes petting pups and drinking coffee.
Kevin Qiu is a Materials Science and Engineering student from Seattle, Washington developing his skills in graphics and web management through his tech - focused role at Community Currency.
